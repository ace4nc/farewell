Class of 2026 Volume I — Issue I August 9, 2026

Rumour has it that the Class of 2026 may finally be getting the farewell they've spent months pretending not to care about. Naturally, nobody is confirming anything.

The details remain suspiciously scarce. Several students have suddenly become very interested in conversations that don't concern them, and a remarkable number of people seem convinced they know exactly what's happening despite having absolutely no evidence to support their claims.

As usual, the rumours are doing what rumours do best: spreading faster than facts.

The people who know what's happening aren't talking.

The people talking don't know what's happening.

From what we've heard, preparations are already underway behind closed doors. The people involved have remained frustratingly secretive, which, if history has taught us anything, usually means something interesting is worth keeping quiet.

Not that anyone is complaining.

After all, half the excitement comes from trying to figure it out. The other half comes from pretending you already have it figured out.

For now, all that's certain is that something is coming. And judging by the amount of speculation it's already generating, it might just be worth the wait.

Important Notice
Regarding the Farewell

We would like to clearly inform everyone that this farewell is NOT an official school event and is in no way associated with the school administration, school management, teachers, or any member of the school staff.

This event is being organised entirely by students for students. Similar student-led farewells have been a long-standing tradition in our school for many years, and this year's event is no different.

Before registering or attending, please ensure that you have your parent(s)' or guardian(s)' full permission. If your parent(s) are not comfortable with you attending, we respectfully request that you do not attend.

Under no circumstances should anyone misrepresent this event as an official school function or tell their parents that it is being organised, supervised, endorsed, or approved by the school, because that would be false.

The organising committee has worked extensively with the venue to ensure proper arrangements and security. Entry will be restricted strictly to invited members of our batch through a verified guest list and physical invitations. Individuals not on the approved guest list will not be permitted to enter the venue.

Appropriate security measures will be in place both inside and outside the venue. However, attendance at this event is entirely voluntary and at each attendee's and their parent(s)' discretion. By choosing to attend, participants acknowledge that they are doing so as private individuals and not as representatives of the school.

The organising committee will make every reasonable effort to ensure a safe and well-managed event, but cannot assume responsibility beyond the arrangements being provided for the event itself.

We kindly request everyone to act responsibly, communicate honestly with their parents, and help ensure that neither the school nor any student organiser is placed in an uncomfortable position due to misunderstandings regarding the nature of this event.
